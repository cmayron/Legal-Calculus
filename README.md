LCES README — How to Use

Legal Calculus Educational System
Procedural‑Literacy Operating System
The record is the case.

---

Purpose and Scope

LCES is a public educational toolkit that teaches how legal systems behave in practice and how to build records that survive review. It is an operational system for structuring filings, preserving reviewable issues, and preventing procedural drift. It is not legal advice and does not create an attorney‑client relationship.

---

Quick Rules

• The record is the case.
• Proposed orders create ruling paths.
• Archive everything.
Follow these three rules consistently; they determine what survives review.


---

How to Use (Core Workflow)

Overview: work on an iPad for thinking and iteration, use Copilot Pro as the reasoning engine, finalize and archive on a desktop.

Device roles

• iPad — Thinking workspace: open the repo, read, annotate, copy the smallest complete unit, paste into Copilot Pro, and iterate.
• Desktop — Construction and commit: finalize drafts, integrate Inspector AI output, commit to the repo, and archive packets.
• iPhone — Quick reference: access checklists, templates, and on‑the‑go notes.


Core loop

1. Retrieve — open the repo and select the smallest complete conceptual block.
2. Think — paste into Copilot Pro and request one focused action: explain; map; extract skeleton; rewrite as a Minimum‑Viable Filing; generate a Blueprint plan.
3. Transform — convert the Blueprint into a short draft: facts → reasons → one‑sentence ask → proposed order.
4. Evaluate — run a red‑team pass (Inspector AI or human): sequencing, pin cites, jurisdiction, negative treatment, and service.
5. Commit — finalize on desktop, commit with metadata, and archive the packet to /packets/.


Example prompts for Copilot Pro

• Rewrite as Minimum‑Viable Filing: “Convert this block into caption; one‑sentence ask; one paragraph facts; one paragraph reasons; signature; certificate of service.”
• Extract doctrine map: “List the three preservation steps and the filing sequence for this issue.”
• Stress test proposed order: “What procedural objections could defeat this proposed order?”


---

Multi‑AI Roles and Operational Loop

Roles

• Blueprint AI — structure, maps, doctrine plans.
• Builder AI — draft and implement from the blueprint.
• Inspector AI — red‑team, test, validate.
• Human — strategy, judgment, final decisions.


Loop: Retrieve → Think → Build → Evaluate → Commit.
Keep human judgment as the final gate: AI assists structure and drafting; humans decide what enters the record.

---

Archive, Metadata, and Practical Tips

Archive best practices

• Save receipts and timestamps (EFSP receipt, screenshots, email copy).
• Commit to /packets/ on desktop with metadata: author; date/time; source file#hash; action; crisis Y/N.
• Encrypt sensitive packets when emailing; avoid public Wi‑Fi.


Metadata fields to include

• Author; Date/Time; Source; Action; Case caption; Crisis flag.


Practical tips

• Always attach a proposed order when asking for relief.
• Keep filings short and focused: one clear request, one paragraph facts, one paragraph reasons.
• Remove or redact PII before pasting into cloud AI.
• Use the smallest complete unit when copying into Copilot Pro to reduce noise and improve output quality.
